# Appendix I: Bond Technicalities

## Core Idea
This appendix translates bond mechanics into practical debt understanding. Slater explains how prices, coupons, maturities, redemption options, and QE-related holdings affect the behavior and interpretation of public debt.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Bond price-rate inverse relationship**:
  - When to use: when government bond prices move sharply.
  - How: remember that higher market rates lower existing bond prices and vice versa.
- **Maturity and redemption structure**:
  - When to use: when comparing seemingly similar debt instruments.
  - How: inspect duration, call features, and certainty of income streams.

## Key Concepts
- Coupon
- Redemption
- Duration

## Mental Models
- Debt management is partly the art of shaping maturity and rate exposure.
- A bond is a stream of payments, not just a face value.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating all gilts as economically identical.
- Ignoring price sensitivity to interest-rate movements.

## Worked Example
Slater walks through how long-dated bonds, index-linked bonds, strips, and callable structures behave differently under changing rates and expectations.

## Key Takeaways
1. Bond mechanics matter for debt burden and market risk.
2. Maturity structure changes refinancing exposure.
3. Technical details often drive policy meaning.
