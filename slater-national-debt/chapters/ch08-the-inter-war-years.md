# Chapter 8: The Inter-War Years

## Core Idea
The inter-war period is a study in debt overhang, unstable adjustment, and the difficulty of restoring normality after a debt-and-reparations shock.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Overhang after war**:
  - When to use: when debt burdens constrain recovery.
  - How: track debt, reparations, inflation, and policy rigidity together.
- **Deflationary pain vs debt relief**:
  - When to use: when governments resist inflation but face heavy inherited debts.
  - How: ask who bears the burden under hard-money adjustment.

## Key Concepts
- Reparations
- Deflation
- Inter-war instability

## Mental Models
- Debt problems persist when postwar settlements are politically unrealistic.
- Stabilization can be socially harsh even when it looks financially respectable.

## Anti-patterns
- Assuming peace automatically restores debt normality.
- Treating inherited debt burdens as mere accounting entries.

## Worked Example
Slater shows how the debts and claims left by WWI obstructed adjustment and contributed to an economically and politically brittle inter-war order.

## Key Takeaways
1. Debt overhang can damage peace-time stabilization.
2. International debt politics matter as much as domestic arithmetic.
3. Inter-war fragility helps explain later policy shifts.
